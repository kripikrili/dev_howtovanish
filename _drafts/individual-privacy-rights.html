Individual privacy rights is the ability of an individual or a group to seclude themselves or information about themselves to the public. Every individual has the right to privacy. What differs from one person to another may be cultural but at the end share the same themes. Expectation of Privacy

There are two ways which you can expect individual privacy rights in our day to day life;

Subjective expectation of privacy - This is where someone’s opinion on a certain location or situation is deemed private. Reasonable expectation of privacy – This expectation is generally recognized by the society. This may include someone’s residence, hotel rooms or public places that are secluded or provided by the public sector.

Generally one sometimes cannot have a reasonable expectation of something held in public trust e.g. financial records held by the bank, garbage left at a central place for collection, fingerprints used by arms of law in conducting investigations or when processing vital documents like identity cards or passports, driving licenses, birth certificates - in  few words privacy and public anonymity.

Civil Liberties

This are rights that provide an individual specific rights e.g. right to life, right to defend yourself, right to privacy, freedom of speech, freedom from slavery, freedom of expression, freedom of assembly and association, the right to marry and have a family, freedom of conscience etc. Also to note of importance is that there exists positive rights and negative rights.

Most of the common civil liberties include freedom of expression, freedom of religion and the right to due process, to own property and privacy. Many countries have established bill of rights that’s ensuring civil liberties are guaranteed. It’s the responsibility of the citizens of the particular country to protect their civil liberties.

There are also contentious civil liberties some of which include property rights; reproductive rights, civil marriage and right to bear arms. Sometimes also when there is a civil strife or state of emergency, debate arises as to what extent the civil liberties can be upheld.

Some civil liberties may also conflict with government laws or when abused e.g. civil to bear arms. If it is abused for other purposes e.g. theft then it conflicts with the right itself as someone else may suffer as a result.
