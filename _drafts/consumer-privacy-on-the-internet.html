With the growing and widening boundaries of freedom culture, people are losing their grip on consumer privacy on the internet. It has always been a matter of fact that unless we are trapped in one, we clearly understand what and how valuable it is. Privacy is an aptitude of a group or an individual to seclude their personal information and thereby divulge them selectively. The term privacy holds it’s certain boundaries and content and may differ with the every culture.

One’s right to consumer privacy on the internet has been continuously going through controversies. A number of topics are being discussed intensely often in the assemblies throughout the world. The most recent and unforgettable moment of 9-11 has made this a burning topic for every nation. People are bound to think over the public safety and personal rights.

Nature of Right to Privacy

The theory of privacy rights for civilians was at first recognized in the United States at 1890's where the official experts felt a necessity to preserve "individual privacy". Later on this right engaged a prominent place in the international document as it turned into a division of Universal Declaration for Human Rights in year 1948. As a result, it is now a fundamental human right that is recognized by UN Declaration of the Human Rights, International Covenant on Political and Civil Rights along with many other regional and international treaties.

It strengthens human dignity and other fundamental values such as freedom of speech and freedom of association. It has turn out to be a most significant human rights issue in this modern era. In order to strengthen it roots we should take care of the following issues:

1. Individual: This form of privacy is termed as bodily privacy'. This level is concerned to the integrity of an individual's body. Issues comprise blood transfusion without consent, compulsory immunization, and compulsory sterilization along with compulsory provision of body fluids along with body tissue.

2. Personal behavior: This level relates to all aspects of behavior, and especially to certain sensitive matters, like sexual habits and preferences, religious practices and political activities, both in public and private places. Media privacy is also a part of this privacy.

3. Personal data: People claim that their data should not be mechanically available to other organizations and individuals and one should implement a substantial level of control over the data and its utilizations. This is also termed as information privacy' and 'd privacy'.

4. Personal communications: People around the world claim an interest to be able to establish contact among themselves by the use of various media, with no custom monitoring of communications by any individual or organization. This level is termed as interception privacy.

So far right of privacy has been demoted to a penumbral position and has never benefited the status of a right. Time has come for lawmakers to enact laws in order to defend consumer privacy on the internet rather than introducing laws that license interruption into private affairs.
