Political privacy issues are existing since the beginning of the different systems of voting. The secret ballot has been the simplest and most diverse measure that has been taken to protect individual political views. Political privacy has been incorporated in most democratic systems and is now regarded as a fundamental right. Politics are majorly about dispute resolution and it put both losers and winners in positions where they can compare themselves to each other.

In a democratic state, the winner is regarded to be vulnerable as he or she often appears in public so as to gain more support. The secret ballot however is not necessarily part of the procedures of legislation. The political privacy issues of the ordinary citizen has always posed some problems.

Political representatives are called upon to provide services to the citizen without placing much importance of whom they voted for in the elections. These requirements would be rendered impossible if the politicians were allowed to go through the votes of the people.

Political privacy issues regarding politics go beyond legislative systems and matters to do with voting. The different opinions given by the citizens can be construed to be having certain political implications. Due to this factor opinion polls are highly protected and therefore do not give the names or details of the people who give such opinions, where there are extreme political opinions that fail to archive an expression that is up to a standard degree in a legal system.

There is always the problem of having to balance the system as opposed to the views of the people. An example of a situation that represents this issue is the wearing of masks during demonstrations and protests. Although this may be termed as a disguise, and the demonstrators more often than not break the laws during a riot, the government has always tolerated the right to express such political views.

Many states have legislated laws against wearing of masks in demonstrations. The higher the infringement the easier it becomes to accuse innocent persons using the differences of their individual views.

Critiques have argued that political privacy that extends beyond voting should be rendered inappropriate. In this regard it may be said that one does not possess any political privacy rights and any measures taken such as the secret ballot may be denied. The basis behind this may be that the different political views between neighbours must be kept open, in order to avoid vesting too much power on one authority.

The opinions of anonymous authors have been widely accepted and have been proved to counter the effects of group thinking and classification of different mind-sets. The political privacy issue of having people group their opinions or classify them as to belong to certain groups poses very risky situations.

It has also been found that the more anonymous an individual remains, the more difficult it is to infringe upon his rights as regards political privacy. It is important that there should be a standard method of regulating people’s political privacy, so that peace in a country may prevail and individuals will be protected from discriminations based on their personal political affiliations and opinions.
