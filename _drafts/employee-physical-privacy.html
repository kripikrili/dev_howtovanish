Learn to protect employee physical privacy. ---

Employee physical privacy can be termed as putting a stop in accessing ones space or seclusion.

This includes such concerns and is not limited to:

1. Preventing unauthorized searches on your body by security agencies for purpose of identifying or heightening security.

2. Preventing unauthorized searched on your home, office or car.

3. The right to make fundamental medical decisions independently without government duress or other third parties especially during questions to do with contraception. This is referred to as medical privacy.

4. Preventing close acts or hiding one's body from others for the purpose of modesty.

5. Showing of video pertinently tagged as graphic or intimate acts that tend to depict sexual acts.
Internet and Privacy

With the emergent use of Internet on our daily life, privacy has become a problem as information regarding oneself is easily accessible through the Internet. But you can protect employee physical privacy and identity by taking simple steps.

Social Networking

In the recent times we have a seen an upsurge growth in the use of social networks e.g. Google+, Twitter, and Facebook. These social networks provide most information about us to the public, recruiters, government agency etc.

The social networks sell your personal information to the highest bidder without your knowledge and rake in billions of dollars from that information. They are powerful tools for change and many organization and bodies would want have access to such information.

But what happens when you give fake information on the social networks? The social networks terms and conditions state that you cannot falsify the information you provide. So for people who do not follow the company terms and privacy policies risk having their accounts shut down?

Cell Phones

Every day more and more sophisticated cell phones are manufactured in the name of smart phones. While we want to be technologically savvy, we risk exposing our camera cell phone privacy to other parties. The smart phones collect a lot of information regarding your privacy such as Account information, contacts information, demographic information, location of the user and number. This vital information is collected without your knowledge and used by other third parties in getting your privacy information.

Pretexting

Other ways you may lose your employee physical privacy is through pretexting. This is when someone accesses your information through false pretenses. This person may require your financial information for a number of reasons and this could lead to losing your money through your bank account, your financial information sold to a share brokerage firm or used against you in a court of law.
Protect yourself by not giving out your information through your phone, contact details on the web or mail. Protesters get the information through false pretense as survey firm, ISPs, bank etc.

Ghost address

With use of private address, your employee physical privacy will be limited to the public. Rent a public place, apartment, house, or condo instead of buying one. When you rent an apartment most of your information will be hidden as rental records are not public. But if you purchase the apartment then your property records will be public for searches.

You may also want to own a property through an LLC other than private name. When you hold a property through LLC your information will not be public as your name will not appear under the records.

You may also want to own property through trust. In this way you can purchase several parcels of land adjacent to one another without the owner’s knowledge. Always use Ghost an address while giving out your address, address lookup is one of the most used databases to get personal information.
