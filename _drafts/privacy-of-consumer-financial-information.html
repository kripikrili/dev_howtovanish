How to protect privacy of consumer financial information. ---

Privacy of consumer financial information refers to the use of information provided to financial institution that in turn sell the information to other companies e.g. telemarketers for marketing purposes. Full financial privacy prohibits the selling or usage of consumer privacy information to other companies, or affiliates for marketing purposes, telemarketing or soliciting customers without their knowledge.

However, sometimes privacy of consumer financial information becomes an issue when companies holding consumer information share the information with other affiliated companies or holding companies. This becomes a thin line if and whether financial privacy has been abused.

Use of Cash

Protecting your privacy of consumer financial information is your own choice. One way to achieve this is by use of cash. Use cash for as much transaction as possible. When you use cash for your transaction the paper trails vanish into ambiguity. It’s not possible to link your transactions to other records of a merchant.

When you use cash the transaction that appear in your statements are either withdrawals or deposits, no information is relayed on where the money came from or is being sent. Unlike credit cards that show your full transactions on the statement, the more you use cash, the fewer the information that is exposed to other third parties. One of the most effective way of to protect your financial privacy is by use of cash.

Use of Bitcoin

Bitcoin is a digital currency that is used to protect financial privacy. We advise you to read how to obtain financial privacy through using BitCoin. It aims at fixing the problem of centralization and in the process help the users to control their financial privacy. With centralization the power is left to an industry regulator like the central bank. With peer-to-peer kind of operation it leaves no chance of centralized authority or a third party institution or person to come into play with the primary currency.

This decentralization sets Bitcoin to stand apart from other forms of digital currencies.Bitcoin are not issued or controlled by a central authority.  Consumers are able to get through software which normally is open source. This means everyone can cheaply obtain bitcoin and do transactions securely while at the same time maintaining the identity of the user.

Bitcoin use extremely encrypted protocols that minimize the risk of the user being exposed to hackers and fraudsters. But this in turn has been blamed for difficulty in using digital currency. But newer applications are being developed that has eased the use of Bitcoin e.g. Gmail by Google.

The transaction that is done using Bitcoin is almost instantaneous and very little amounts can be transacted with ease. The digital currency can then be exchanged to hard currency via other applications e.g. Bitinstant. It’s extremely difficult for central governments to control digital currencies. And because it uses open source software it’s extremely difficult to stop Bitcoin and therefore offers privacy of consumer financial information like no other form of digital currency.

In conclusion despite financial privacy being a fundamental right to people, there many other forces outside there that are trying to erode the gains that have been made. But free and open source software like Bitcoin provide an essential decentralized digitally protected currency that’s hard to hack.

Always take the initiative to protect your financial privacy by using digital currency like Bitcoin.
