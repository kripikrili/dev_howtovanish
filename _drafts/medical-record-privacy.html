HIPAA Privacy Laws

HIPAA was enacted by the united stated congress in 1996 and signed the same year by the sitting president at that time Bill Clinton. It serves to protect health insurance coverage for workers and their families when they lose their jobs or change jobs.

It also provides for an establishment of electronic health care transactions and national identifiers for providers, health insurance plans and employers.

It also regulates the availability and composition of group health plans and certain individual health insurance policies.
The HIPAA privacy rule regulates the use and disclosure of protected health information (PHI) held by covered entities which include medical insurance providers, health insurers, health care clearinghouses and sponsored health plans by some employers.

The medical record privacy rule gives individuals the right to request that a covered entity correct any errors in PHI.
It also requires entities to ensure private information of all communications between the covered entity and the individuals.It also requires that that covered entities provide information or notify individuals on their PHI.

An individual who feels that his personal information to health records is infringed upon can file a complaint with the Department of Health and Human Services Office of civil rights.
